# Usage Patterns

## Ordinary Output

For short captions, greetings, community messages, and tone adaptation, produce usable Minangkabau-oriented output when local resources cover the wording. Keep a short note only for uncertain adat, dialect, or public-use terms.

## Sample Tasks

- Make a Minangkabau-flavored caption for a community event.
- Review whether text sounds like Indonesian with Minang words.
- Create a bilingual Indonesian-Minangkabau announcement.
- Adjust tone for rantau audience versus local nagari audience.
- Flag adat and kinship terms that need review.

## Register And Semantics

- Ask whether the target is everyday, public, adat/community, youth, rantau, or a specific local area.
- Do not overuse stereotypes such as trading, food, or merantau when irrelevant.
- Preserve social hierarchy and kinship intent.
- Keep legal, medical, technical, and institutional terms in Indonesian unless a verified local equivalent is known.

## Review Checklist

- Does the output confuse Minangkabau with generic West Sumatran Indonesian?
- Are adat and kinship terms handled with context?
- Are dialect/community assumptions marked?
- Are names, titles, dates, and facts unchanged?
- Does the answer avoid claiming native or official verification?

## Output Patterns

- Direct request: output first, review note only when needed.
- Review: list interference, risky terms, and safer wording.
- Public message: bilingual output plus review note for adat/dialect terms.
