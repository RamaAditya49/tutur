# Style Guide

## Language Identity

- Name: Bahasa Minangkabau
- Region/context: Sumatra Barat, rantau Minangkabau, dan komunitas Minang
- Glottocode: `mina1268`
- ISO 639-3: `min`

## Register Position

Bahasa Minangkabau has strong regional identity and dialect variation. Do not reduce it to "bahasa Padang" or generic Malay/Indonesian with Minang particles.

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent vocabulary.
- Do not assume one dialect represents all speakers.
- Do not assume all Minangkabau speakers use the same local form.
- Do not use the language for sacred, ritual, legal, or official text without review.
- Preserve Indonesian terms when the regional equivalent is unknown.
- Ask for a user-provided sample when voice or dialect matters.

## Practical Use

Use this skill for:

- source-aware regional-language output,
- tone adaptation,
- localized greetings or short public text,
- review of AI-generated local-language output,
- identifying uncertainty before publication.

## Practical Notes

- Ask whether the target is public Minang, rantau tone, village/local dialect, or ceremonial adat context.
- Be source-aware with adat, kinship, title, and religious language.
- Keep Indonesian terms for modern technical vocabulary unless a verified Minangkabau term is available.
