# Usage Patterns

## Ordinary Output

For everyday, polite, and short public Sundanese text, produce the output directly once the speech level is clear. If the speech level is unknown, ask a short question or choose a safe public-politeness level and label it.

## Sample Tasks

- Rewrite a school announcement in polite Bahasa Sunda.
- Review whether a Sundanese message mixes loma and lemes.
- Make casual and polite variants for the same caption.
- Localize a greeting for a Sundanese community event.
- Flag pronouns or address terms that feel risky.

## Register And Semantics

- Loma: everyday/familiar; not for all public or elder-facing contexts.
- Lemes: polite/respectful; useful for public and elder-facing wording.
- Very respectful: use only when the social relationship is clear.
- Youth/casual: keep it natural, not forced slang.
- Dialect: ask for area when the user names a local target.

## Review Checklist

- Is the speech level consistent?
- Are pronouns and address terms appropriate?
- Does the text preserve the original meaning?
- Are Indonesian technical terms intentionally retained?
- Does the answer avoid pretending to represent every Sundanese dialect?

## Output Patterns

- Direct request: output first, label the level.
- Variant request: provide loma and lemes versions.
- Review: list level mismatches, then safer rewrite.
