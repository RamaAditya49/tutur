# Style Guide

## Language Identity

- Name: Bahasa Sunda
- Region/context: Jawa Barat, Banten, dan komunitas Sunda di wilayah lain
- Glottocode: `sund1252`
- ISO 639-3: `sun`

## Register Position

Bahasa Sunda has speech-level sensitivity. Before writing, identify whether the user wants casual/loma, polite/lemes, very respectful, public announcement style, or a specific local dialect. Do not mix levels casually.

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent vocabulary.
- Do not assume one dialect represents all speakers.
- Do not flatten all Sundanese into Bandung-style everyday speech.
- Do not use informal forms for elders, formal announcements, official settings, or religious/cultural events unless requested.
- Do not use the language for sacred, ritual, legal, or official text without review.
- Preserve Indonesian terms when the regional equivalent is unknown.
- Ask for a user-provided sample when voice or dialect matters.

## Practical Use

Use this skill for:

- source-aware regional-language output,
- tone adaptation,
- localized greetings or short public text,
- review of AI-generated local-language output,
- identifying uncertainty before publication.

## Practical Notes

- Ask for target audience when politeness matters.
- Be careful with pronouns and address terms.
- For Banten/Baduy/Kanekes or Cirebon-border contexts, ask for the specific variety instead of assuming mainstream Priangan Sunda.
