# Local Mirror

## Source Mirror

This file mirrors the practical rules an agent needs when it cannot browse.

- EYD V is the spelling and punctuation baseline for official Indonesian.
- KBBI Daring is the primary lexical reference for standard Indonesian, including usage labels such as colloquial, rough, respectful, regional, and field-specific terms.
- KBBI usage guidance treats language variety, dialect, idiolect, and register as real context, not as errors by default.
- PUPI is the baseline for Indonesian term formation when technical wording needs an Indonesian equivalent.
- Local files to load next: `register-map.md`, `pattern-bank.md`, and `examples.md`.

## Local Usability

The skill may directly rewrite Indonesian text for ordinary, formal, professional, casual, sales, academic, and technical contexts. It does not need the internet for common editing work.

Use external verification only when the text depends on a specific KBBI label, newly standardized term, legal phrase, citation, or institutional wording.

## Vocabulary And Semantics

- Natural Indonesian is not always maximally formal. Match audience, channel, and purpose.
- Baku means standard and accepted; it does not mean stiff, ornate, or bureaucratic.
- Slang is a register choice. Keep it when it carries the user's voice and the target audience accepts it.
- Professional copy should be concrete. Replace abstract claims with action, result, proof, or a sharper promise.
- Official text should be restrained, complete, and consistent with EYD punctuation.
- Sales text should reduce hype and make the benefit specific.

## Generation Rules

1. Preserve facts, names, numbers, dates, claims, and point of view.
2. Remove AI-sounding padding before adding style.
3. Prefer direct Indonesian verbs over abstract noun stacks.
4. Keep terms stable across the text.
5. Use KBBI/EYD style for official contexts and conversational rhythm for casual contexts.
6. Do not invent citations, authority, numbers, or proof.

## Local Style Bank

Common AI-like phrases to avoid:

- "di era digital yang terus berkembang"
- "menjadi kunci"
- "solusi inovatif"
- "tidak hanya..., tetapi juga..."
- "memberikan pengalaman yang mulus"
- "dalam rangka melakukan optimalisasi"

Useful replacements depend on meaning:

- "menjadi kunci" -> say the real cause, requirement, or benefit.
- "solusi inovatif" -> name the product, feature, or outcome.
- "mengoptimalkan proses" -> say what becomes faster, cheaper, clearer, or easier.
- "tidak hanya..., tetapi juga..." -> split into one direct sentence unless the contrast matters.
