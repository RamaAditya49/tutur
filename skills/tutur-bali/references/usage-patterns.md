# Usage Patterns

## Ordinary Output

For everyday captions, visitor-facing messages, and short community texts, produce a direct output once the speech level and audience are clear. Add notes only when adat, temple, or status-sensitive wording is involved.

## Sample Tasks

- Make a polite Balinese visitor greeting.
- Review whether a Balinese text mixes speech levels.
- Create everyday and more respectful variants.
- Localize a tourism caption without ritual claims.
- Flag temple/adat terms that need expert review.

## Register And Semantics

- Everyday: simple community communication.
- Polite/respectful: elder-facing, public, and customer-facing contexts.
- Adat/temple/ritual: high caution; use expert review.
- Tourism: clear and respectful, not falsely ceremonial.
- Dialect/local: ask for Nusa Penida, Bali Aga, or other local target when needed.

## Review Checklist

- Is the speech level consistent?
- Are sacred/adat terms avoided unless needed?
- Are status-sensitive address choices handled carefully?
- Are Indonesian technical terms preserved when no verified equivalent is known?
- Does the output avoid claiming ritual authenticity?

## Output Patterns

- Direct request: output first, level/context label if helpful.
- Review: level and adat risks first, then safer rewrite.
- Public/temple text: conservative bilingual output plus review note.
