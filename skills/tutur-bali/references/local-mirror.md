## Vocabulary And Semantics

- `Anggah-ungguh` is central. Ordinary words, respectful words, and ritual/adat terms are not interchangeable.
- Do not use sacred or temple formulas as decoration.
- Tourism-facing Indonesian-Balinese text can be simpler than adat/ritual text.
- Nusa Penida, Bali Aga, and diaspora contexts may require local samples.
- Keep Indonesian for legal, medical, technical, and institutional terms unless a verified Balinese equivalent is known.

## Generation Rules

1. Identify context: everyday, polite, tourism, adat, temple, ceremony, or local dialect.
2. For ordinary messages, produce concise output with level-aware wording.
3. For ritual/adat contexts, keep uncertain terms marked and recommend review.
4. Preserve names, place names, titles, dates, and facts.
5. Avoid over-ornamenting text to sound "traditional".

## Starter Lexicon Policy
