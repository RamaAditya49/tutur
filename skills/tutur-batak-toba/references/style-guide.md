# Style Guide

## Language Identity

- Name: Bahasa Batak Toba
- Region/context: Sumatera Utara: kawasan Toba/Samosir/Tapanuli Utara dan komunitas Batak Toba diaspora; berbeda dari Karo, Simalungun, Mandailing, Angkola, dan Pakpak

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent vocabulary.
- Do not assume one dialect represents all speakers.
- Do not use the language for sacred, ritual, legal, or official text without review.
- Preserve Indonesian terms when the regional equivalent is unknown.
- Ask for a user-provided sample when voice or dialect matters.
- Do not assign kinship terms without knowing the relationship.
- Do not generate marga, adat, church, wedding, ulos, ritual, or Batak script text from scratch.
- Keep policy, billing, legal, medical, school, and government details in Indonesian unless verified Batak Toba wording is supplied.

## Practical Use

Use this skill for:

- source-aware regional-language output,
- tone adaptation,
- localized greetings or short public text,
- review of AI-generated local-language output,
- identifying uncertainty before publication.

## Registers

### Everyday/Casual

Use short Batak Toba cues with clear Indonesian:

- `Horas`
- `Mauliate`
- `Hata Batak Toba`

Suitable for casual captions, learning prompts, and community greetings.

### Polite/Community

Use address terms only when relationship is known:

> Horas, Bapak/Ibu. Mauliate sudah hadir pada kegiatan komunitas hari ini.

Avoid guessing `Amang`, `Inang`, `Lae`, `Ito`, `Tulang`, or adat relation terms unless the user gives context.

### Professional/Admin

For admin, school, sales, CS, finance, tourism, church-event logistics, or government-adjacent work:

- keep operational facts in Indonesian,
- use Batak Toba only as checked local warmth,
- avoid full translation without user sample.

### Adat/Ceremonial

Do not generate adat, wedding, marga, ulos, church, ritual, or Batak script text from scratch. Ask for source text from the community and use this skill only to review or flag uncertainty.
