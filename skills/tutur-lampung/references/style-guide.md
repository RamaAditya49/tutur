# Style Guide

## Language Identity

- Name: Bahasa Lampung
- Region/context: Provinsi Lampung dan komunitas Lampung diaspora; perhatikan variasi dialek Api/A dan Nyo/O serta aksara Lampung

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent vocabulary.
- Do not assume one dialect represents all speakers.
- Do not use the language for sacred, ritual, legal, or official text without review.
- Preserve Indonesian terms when the regional equivalent is unknown.
- Ask for a user-provided sample when voice or dialect matters.
- Ask whether the target is Lampung Api/A, Lampung Nyo/O, or a local community form.
- Do not mix Api/A and Nyo/O forms unless the user explicitly wants comparison.
- Do not generate aksara Lampung for public use without source review.

## Practical Use

Use this skill for:

- source-aware regional-language output,
- tone adaptation,
- localized greetings or short public text,
- review of AI-generated local-language output,
- identifying uncertainty before publication.

## Registers

### Everyday/Casual

Use short dialect-labelled phrases:

- Api/A: `Api kabar?`
- Nyo/O: `Nyow kabar?`
- Response: `Kabar wawai.`

### Polite/Public

Use `Tabik pun` sparingly with Indonesian support text:

> Tabik pun. Selamat datang di kegiatan komunitas Lampung. Informasi jadwal dan lokasi ada di bawah ini.

Review public text before printing or broadcasting.

### Professional/Admin

For admin, school, sales, CS, finance, tourism, or government-adjacent work:

- keep operational facts in Indonesian,
- use Lampung only as checked local warmth,
- avoid full translation without user sample.

### Adat/Script

Do not generate adat, wedding, ritual, or aksara Lampung text from scratch. Ask for source text and local review.
