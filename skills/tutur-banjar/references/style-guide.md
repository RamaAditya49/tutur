# Style Guide

## Language Identity

- Name: Bahasa Banjar
- Region/context: Kalimantan Selatan dan komunitas Banjar di Kalimantan serta diaspora; dialek utama Banjar Kuala dan Banjar Hulu

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent vocabulary.
- Do not assume one dialect represents all speakers.
- Do not use the language for sacred, ritual, legal, or official text without review.
- Preserve Indonesian terms when the regional equivalent is unknown.
- Ask for a user-provided sample when voice or dialect matters.
- Ask whether the user wants Banjar Kuala, Banjar Hulu, or general Banjar when the text is public.
- Keep policy, billing, legal, medical, school, and government details in Indonesian unless the user supplies verified Banjar wording.

## Practical Use

Use this skill for:

- source-aware regional-language output,
- tone adaptation,
- localized greetings or short public text,
- review of AI-generated local-language output,
- identifying uncertainty before publication.

## Registers

### Everyday/Casual

Use short phrases and familiar pronouns when relationship fits:

- `Apa habar?`
- `Ulun`
- `Pian`
- `Bujur`

Suitable for friend chat, community captions, and simple local flavor.

### Polite/Public

Use Banjar phrases sparingly and keep the action clear:

> Apa habar, Bapak/Ibu. Ulun ingatkan rapat lingkungan malam ini pukul 20.00. Mohon hadir tepat waktu.

If uncertain, keep the public message in Indonesian and include only checked Banjar greeting or affirmation.

### Professional/Admin

For admin, office, school, sales, CS, finance, or government-adjacent work:

- keep deadlines, amounts, rules, and documents in Indonesian,
- use Banjar phrases only as greeting or local warmth,
- avoid full translation unless the user supplies a local sample.

### Adat/Ceremonial

Do not create adat, wedding, religious, or formal ceremonial Banjar language from scratch. Ask for a source sample from the community or a competent speaker.
