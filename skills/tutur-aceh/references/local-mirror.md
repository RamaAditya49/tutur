## Vocabulary And Semantics

- Bahasa Aceh is not Indonesian with Acehnese words inserted into Indonesian grammar.
- Treat address terms as semantic choices, not decorations. KBDA sample entries show that terms can carry social relation or regional notes.
- Known sample from KBDA page A: `abu` can be a male elder/fatherly address in some Aceh regions; do not apply it generically to everyone.
- Known sample from KBDA page A: `ado` is an address for a younger person; check context before using it.
- Known sample from KBDA page A: `abè` relates to ash; compounds such as cooking-ash terms show that dictionary entries can be domain-specific.
- The `adat` entry family shows that cultural/legal/community terms can form many compounds; do not translate adat formulas freely.
- Keep Islamic greetings and Arabic-origin religious phrases as the user writes them unless the user asks for localization.
- If the target community is Pidie, Aceh Timur, Aceh Besar, coastal Aceh, or diaspora, ask for local sample when phrasing matters.
