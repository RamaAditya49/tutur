# Usage Patterns

## Ordinary Output

For everyday greetings, captions, short community messages, and review tasks, produce usable output when local resources or user samples cover the wording. If the exact Acehnese wording is not covered, keep that part in Indonesian and mark it briefly.

## Sample Tasks

- Review whether a text sounds like Bahasa Aceh or Indonesian with Acehnese words.
- Adapt a short Indonesian greeting for an Acehnese community audience.
- Make a bilingual Indonesian-Bahasa Aceh poster caption with uncertain terms marked.
- Check address terms such as elder/younger references.
- Identify terms that need KBDA or speaker verification.

## Register And Semantics

- Ask for audience and community when address terms, adat terms, or public event wording matters.
- Keep religious greetings stable unless asked otherwise.
- Use bilingual output for public contexts when confidence is limited: Indonesian first, Aceh-supported terms second.
- Do not force a single regional form as all-Aceh usage.

## Review Checklist

- Does the text use Indonesian word order with only Acehnese substitutions?
- Are address terms socially appropriate?
- Are adat, religious, legal, or educational terms handled conservatively?
- Are uncertain terms left in Indonesian or marked for verification?
- Does the output avoid claiming Balai Bahasa/native authority?

## Output Patterns

- Direct short text: provide output first, then one review note if needed.
- Review request: list interference, uncertain terms, and a safer rewrite.
- Public text: provide bilingual wording and note what needs speaker review.
