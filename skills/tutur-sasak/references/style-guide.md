# Style Guide

## Language Identity

- Name: Bahasa Sasak
- Region/context: Pulau Lombok, Nusa Tenggara Barat; sebaran diaspora di Bali, Sulawesi Tenggara, dan Lampung menurut Peta Bahasa

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent vocabulary.
- Do not assume one dialect represents all speakers.
- Do not use the language for sacred, ritual, legal, or official text without review.
- Preserve Indonesian terms when the regional equivalent is unknown.
- Ask for a user-provided sample when voice or dialect matters.
- Ask for the target dialect when the user names a village, district, adat group, or speaker community.
- Use public Indonesian for operational facts such as prices, safety notices, schedules, policy, permits, and legal details unless the user supplies verified Sasak.

## Practical Use

Use this skill for:

- source-aware regional-language output,
- tone adaptation,
- localized greetings or short public text,
- review of AI-generated local-language output,
- identifying uncertainty before publication.

## Registers

### Everyday/Casual

Use short phrases and plain rhythm:

- `Berembe kabar?`
- `Matur tampiasih.`
- `Tabeq.`

Suitable for friend chat, visitor greeting, community captions, and simple reminders.

### Polite/Public

Use short verified Sasak phrases with Indonesian support text:

> Tabeq. Selamat datang di Lombok. Untuk informasi jadwal dan lokasi acara, silakan cek papan pengumuman.

This avoids pretending the whole public announcement is a certified Sasak translation.

### Professional/Tourism

For tourism, hospitality, admin, school, or office contexts:

- keep facts in Indonesian,
- add one or two safe Sasak greetings,
- avoid long unsupported Sasak paragraphs,
- mark formal-print wording for speaker review.

### Adat/Ceremonial

Do not generate adat formulas, ritual greetings, marriage text, religious formulas, or status-sensitive address from scratch. Ask for a source sample from the local speaker/community.

## Dialect Notes

Peta Bahasa describes four phonological dialect variants: `[a-a]`, `[a-â]`, `[â-â]`, and `[a-o]`. Do not force one spelling system for all contexts. If the user's text targets Bayan, Sembalun, Pringgasela, Lombok Tengah, Lombok Barat, Lombok Timur, or diaspora communities, ask whether a local form is preferred.
