# Local Mirror

## Source Mirror

- Language/register: Bahasa Jawa.
- Region/context: Jawa Tengah, DI Yogyakarta, Jawa Timur, diaspora Jawa, and related local varieties.
- Broad family context: Austronesian / Malayo-Polynesian.
- Glottolog: Javanese, glottocode `java1254`.
- ISO 639-3: `jav`.
- Public references include Javanese dictionary and descriptive grammar resources from Kemendikdasmen repositories.
- Everyday Bahasa Jawa is separate from the `tutur-kedhaton-solo` and `tutur-bagongan-jogja` court-register skills.

## Local Usability

Use this local mirror before browsing. The skill can produce ordinary Javanese output, review register consistency, and create short public messages when the requested level is clear.

For ritual, palace, manuscript, wedding-ceremony, legal, or government text, use expert review and consider whether a court-register skill is more appropriate.

## Vocabulary And Semantics

- Speech level is central: ngoko, krama, krama inggil, public polite, and local dialect choices are not interchangeable.
- Do not mix ngoko, krama, and krama inggil as decoration.
- Pronouns and verbs often change with respect level; avoid direct pronouns when the relationship is unclear.
- East Javanese, Central Javanese, Yogyakarta, Banyumasan, and diaspora contexts can differ.
- Keep Indonesian institutional, legal, health, and technical terms when no verified Javanese term is known.

## Generation Rules

1. Identify target level: ngoko, krama, krama inggil, public polite, dialect, or court-related.
2. Route court-specific Solo/Jogja requests to the appropriate court skill.
3. Preserve names, titles, dates, places, and facts.
4. Use plain Indonesian for uncertain technical terms.
5. Add a short note for public or level-sensitive contexts.

## Starter Lexicon Policy

Do not bulk-copy dictionaries. Add verified level-paired terms only when their level is clear. Prioritize full-sentence patterns because isolated Javanese words can break register consistency.
