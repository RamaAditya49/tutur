# Style Guide

## Language Identity

- Name: Bahasa Jawa
- Region/context: Jawa Tengah, DI Yogyakarta, Jawa Timur, diaspora Jawa, dan variasi lokal terkait
- Glottocode: `java1254`
- ISO 639-3: `jav`

## Register Position

Bahasa Jawa has strong speech-level and dialect variation. Do not treat "Jawa" as a single neutral style. Ask whether the target is ngoko, krama, krama inggil, Mataraman, Banyumasan/Ngapak, Suroboyoan, Osing, Tengger, Cirebonan, or another local context when it matters.

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent vocabulary.
- Do not assume one dialect represents all speakers.
- Do not use ngoko for elders, formal events, public notices, or respectful requests unless the user asks for it.
- Do not mix court-register Kedhaton/Bagongan choices into ordinary Bahasa Jawa unless requested.
- Do not use the language for sacred, ritual, legal, or official text without review.
- Preserve Indonesian terms when the regional equivalent is unknown.
- Ask for a user-provided sample when voice or dialect matters.

## Practical Use

Use this skill for:

- cautious regional-language drafts,
- tone adaptation,
- localized greetings or short public text,
- review of AI-generated local-language output,
- identifying uncertainty before publication.

## Practical Notes

- Ask for target audience and speech level before drafting sensitive text.
- Be careful with pronouns, verbs for eating/going/saying, and kinship terms.
- For Keraton Solo or Keraton Jogja, prefer the dedicated skills `tutur-kedhaton-solo` or `tutur-bagongan-jogja`.
