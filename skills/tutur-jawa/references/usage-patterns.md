# Usage Patterns

## Ordinary Output

For everyday Javanese, polite public messages, and register review, produce direct output after choosing the speech level. Label the level when useful.

## Sample Tasks

- Make ngoko and krama variants of a short message.
- Rewrite an announcement in polite public Bahasa Jawa.
- Review whether a text mixes ngoko and krama incorrectly.
- Check whether a request should use everyday Javanese or a court-register skill.
- Localize a greeting without changing facts or names.

## Register And Semantics

- Ngoko: familiar, casual, peer/family contexts.
- Krama: polite, public, elder-facing, or formal contexts.
- Krama inggil: respect toward the addressee or referent; use carefully.
- Court registers: use `tutur-kedhaton-solo` or `tutur-bagongan-jogja`.
- Dialect: ask for area when the user needs local authenticity.

## Review Checklist

- Is the chosen level consistent throughout?
- Are honorific forms used toward the right person?
- Are court-register elements avoided unless requested?
- Are Indonesian official/technical terms preserved where needed?
- Are dialect assumptions marked?

## Output Patterns

- Direct request: output first, label level if relevant.
- Variant request: provide ngoko/krama or casual/polite variants.
- Review: level issues first, then safer rewrite.
