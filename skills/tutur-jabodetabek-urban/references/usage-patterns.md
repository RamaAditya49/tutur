# Usage Patterns

## Ordinary Output

For chat, social captions, casual brand copy, and internal casual text, produce the adapted text directly. Keep notes short unless the text risks stereotyping or overdoing code-mix.

## Sample Tasks

- Make a caption sound Jaksel but not cringe.
- Rewrite a team chat in neutral Jabodetabek casual.
- Make a reminder sound like Jaktim casual.
- Make a Bekasi commuter-style update.
- Review whether a code-mixed sentence is natural.
- Create variants: neutral, Jaksel, Jaktim, Bekasi.

## Register And Semantics

- Friend chat can use `gue/lo`, abbreviations, and playful tone.
- Brand caption should be readable by people outside the local group.
- Corporate casual may use English work terms, but the action must stay clear.
- Public service and HR should avoid heavy slang.
- Local identity should be flavor, not stereotype.

## Review Checklist

- Is the target style named?
- Does the text remain understandable?
- Is code-mix controlled?
- Does it avoid mocking a neighborhood/class/ethnic group?
- Is it appropriate for the channel?
- Are jokes and memes user-requested?

## Output Patterns

- Direct request: adapted text first.
- Variants: label each style.
- Review: point out forced code-mix, unclear slang, and stereotype risk.
- Professional context: provide a cleaner corporate-casual option.
