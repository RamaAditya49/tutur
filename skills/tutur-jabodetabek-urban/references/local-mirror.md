# Local Mirror

## Source Mirror

This local mirror turns the research into offline style guidance:

- Jaksel language is commonly described as Indonesian-English code-mixing in youth/social-media speech.
- Studies describe code-mixing as English insertion at word, phrase, and clause levels, with Indonesian remaining the base language.
- Research also links Jaksel-style speech with social identity, peer-group expression, modernity, and social media spread.
- Jakartan Indonesian and Betawi/Jakarta colloquial speech are not identical to formal Indonesian.
- Bekasi has Betawi Malay dialect variation and youth slang influenced by popular culture and group identity.

## Local Usability

Use this file for style adaptation, captions, chat, social content, and review. It is not a dialect dictionary.

The skill may directly write ordinary informal text. For academic, official, legal, HR, public service, or culturally sensitive text, avoid heavy slang and use standard Indonesian unless the user specifically wants a character voice.

## Vocabulary And Semantics

- Jaksel: use code-mix for concepts often discussed in English: `basically`, `literally`, `vibe`, `healing`, `meeting`, `deadline`, `review`, `brief`, `update`, `worth it`, `awkward`, `relate`.
- Corporate Jaksel: `sync`, `align`, `deck`, `scope`, `timeline`, `deliverable`, `feedback`, `blocker`.
- Jaktim/Jakarta casual: use direct rhythm, `gue/lo`, `nih`, `dong`, `aja`, `banget`, `gas`, `ribet`, `beres`.
- Bekasi: use practical commuter rhythm, `otw`, `macet`, `nanti gue update`, `deket`, `aman`, but avoid making Bekasi a punchline.
- Neutral Jabodetabek: common informal Indonesian with light Jakarta particles.

## Generation Rules

1. Keep Indonesian as the semantic base unless the user asks for heavy code-mix.
2. Add only 2-5 marked slang/code-mix signals in short text.
3. Match relationship: friend, coworker, customer, brand audience, or public.
4. Keep jokes opt-in, especially location jokes.
5. Do not turn sociolect into mockery.
6. For professional use, favor clarity over local flavor.

## Style Map

- Jaksel: polished casual, code-mixed, social-media/startup/cafe tone.
- Jaktim: practical, direct, neighborhood casual, less polished.
- Bekasi: practical, commuter-aware, mixed urban edge, not caricature.
- Neutral Jabodetabek: safe casual Indonesian for broad urban audiences.
