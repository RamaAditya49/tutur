# Tutur Jabodetabek Urban

## Overview

Use this skill to write or review informal Indonesian urban styles associated with Jabodetabek. It covers Jaksel-style Indonesian-English code-mix, Jaktim/Jakarta direct casual tone, Bekasi commuter/youth tone, and neutral Jabodetabek chat.

This is a sociolect/style skill, not a claim that all people from a place speak the same way.

## Workflow

1. Identify the target style.
   - Jaksel: Indonesian base with controlled English code-mix.
   - Jaktim/Jakarta casual: direct, practical, conversational, light Betawi/Jakarta flavor.
   - Bekasi: commuter-pragmatic, casual, Jakarta-Bekasi mix, no lazy "planet Bekasi" jokes unless requested.
   - Neutral Jabodetabek: everyday chat/caption that works across Jakarta, Depok, Tangerang, Bekasi, and Bogor.

2. Identify channel and audience.
   - Friend chat: more slang.
   - Brand caption: slang controlled and readable.
   - Corporate casual: code-mix allowed, but keep task clear.
   - Public/inclusive: avoid location stereotypes.

3. Write with restraint.
   - Do not overload slang.
   - Keep meaning clear.
   - Match age, relationship, platform, and topic.
   - Avoid classist, ethnic, or neighborhood mockery.

## Output Style

- For direct requests, return the adapted text first.
- For review, flag overdone code-mix, stereotypes, unclear slang, and tone mismatch.
- For variants, label `neutral`, `Jaksel`, `Jaktim`, `Bekasi`, or `corporate casual`.
