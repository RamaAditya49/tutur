# Local Mirror

## Source Mirror

- Language/register: Bahasa Gayo.
- Region/context: Dataran tinggi Aceh, especially Gayo communities.
- Broad family context: Austronesian / Malayo-Polynesian.
- Glottolog: Gayo, glottocode `gayo1244`.
- ISO 639-3: `gay`.
- KBDA Daring includes a `Kamus Bahasa Gayo` section by Balai Bahasa Provinsi Aceh.
- Badan Bahasa/Kemendikdasmen resource: `Kamus Umum Bahasa Gayo-Indonesia`.
- Balai Bahasa Provinsi Aceh peta bahasa material is shared with Aceh-language mapping references.
- The Aceh peta bahasa repository item lists Gayo as one of the regional languages represented in the province.

## Local Usability

Use this as the offline source orientation for Gayo. Do not treat Gayo as a variety of Aceh; keep the identity separate.

The skill can produce short ordinary-context text, review vocabulary/register risk, and localize community messages when local resources or user examples support the wording. Dialect and ceremonial/community-specific wording should be checked by a competent speaker.

## Vocabulary And Semantics

- Gayo is associated with highland Aceh communities; avoid coastal Acehnese assumptions.
- Do not borrow Acehnese terms just because both languages are represented by Balai Bahasa Aceh resources.
- Kinship, land, adat, coffee/agriculture, and community-event vocabulary can be semantically loaded; use user samples for precision.
- Keep Indonesian administrative, legal, medical, and technical terms when no Gayo equivalent is verified.
- Preserve place names and personal names exactly.

## Generation Rules

1. Identify whether the user wants everyday Gayo, public community tone, or a specific local variety.
2. Build a plain Indonesian meaning baseline.
3. Apply only verified Gayo terms or user-provided samples.
4. Keep uncertain terms in Indonesian and mark them for review.
5. Do not label output as official or native-verified.

## Starter Lexicon Policy

This repo does not bulk-copy the Gayo dictionary. Add only short verified terms and semantic notes as the skill matures. Prefer phrase templates and user-provided samples over invented vocabulary.
