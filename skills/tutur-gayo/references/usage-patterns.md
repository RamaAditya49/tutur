# Usage Patterns

## Ordinary Output

For short messages, captions, community announcements, and review tasks, produce a direct output when the wording is covered by local references or user samples. Keep uncertain parts visible rather than hiding them.

## Sample Tasks

- Make a short Bahasa Gayo community greeting from Indonesian.
- Review whether a text confuses Gayo with Aceh.
- Create an Indonesian-Gayo bilingual caption for a cultural event.
- Check whether kinship or adat terms need speaker review.

## Register And Semantics

- Ask for the target community or area when the user wants a public-facing text.
- Avoid treating all highland Aceh contexts as one voice.
- Preserve Indonesian for technical and official terms unless a verified Gayo equivalent is known.
- Keep the tone modest and direct.

## Review Checklist

- Is Gayo clearly separated from Bahasa Aceh?
- Are local identity terms handled respectfully?
- Are community, adat, or ceremonial phrases marked when uncertain?
- Are names, dates, and locations unchanged?
- Does the answer avoid false official authority?

## Output Patterns

- Direct request: output first, short uncertainty note after.
- Review: risks first, then corrected or safer wording.
- Public text: bilingual option plus speaker-review note for uncertain parts.
