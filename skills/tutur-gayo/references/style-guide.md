# Style Guide

## Language Identity

- Name: Bahasa Gayo
- Region/context: Dataran tinggi Aceh, terutama komunitas Gayo
- Glottocode: `gayo1244`
- ISO 639-3: `gay`

## Register Position

Bahasa Gayo is a regional language with its own identity. Do not treat it as a dialect of Aceh or Indonesian. Keep Aceh-language and Gayo-language choices separate.

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent vocabulary.
- Do not assume one dialect represents all speakers.
- Do not borrow Acehnese forms just because both are in Aceh Province.
- Do not use the language for sacred, ritual, legal, or official text without review.
- Preserve Indonesian terms when the regional equivalent is unknown.
- Ask for a user-provided sample when voice or dialect matters.

## Practical Use

Use this skill for:

- cautious regional-language drafts,
- tone adaptation,
- localized greetings or short public text,
- review of AI-generated local-language output,
- identifying uncertainty before publication.

## Practical Notes

- Use KBDA Daring and the Gayo-Indonesian dictionary for word checks.
- Ask for community/dialect context when the text targets a specific Gayo area.
- Keep Indonesian terms for modern technical vocabulary unless a verified Gayo term is available.
