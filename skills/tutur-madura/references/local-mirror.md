# Local Mirror

## Source Mirror

- Language/register: Bahasa Madura.
- Region/context: Pulau Madura, Tapal Kuda Jawa Timur, Bawean, and Madurese diaspora communities.
- Broad family context: Austronesian / Malayo-Polynesian.
- ISO 639-3: `mad`.
- Common Glottolog reference in this repo: `nucl1460` for Madurese.
- Public references include Badan Bahasa peta bahasa, Madura-Indonesia dictionary material, and Madurese grammar resources.

## Local Usability

Use this local mirror before browsing. The skill can produce ordinary Madurese-oriented output, review register and dialect risk, and make short bilingual public messages when local resources or user samples cover the wording.

For Sumenep prestige tone, Bawean, Tapal Kuda/Pendalungan contexts, ceremonies, or public official messages, mark uncertain terms for speaker review.

## Vocabulary And Semantics

- Bahasa Madura is not a dialect of Bahasa Jawa.
- Dialect and prestige orientation matter: Sumenep, Bangkalan, Pamekasan, Sampang, Bawean, and Tapal Kuda contexts may differ.
- Speech-level and politeness choices can matter; do not assume everyday wording is suitable for elders or public events.
- Preserve Indonesian for legal, health, technical, and institutional terms unless a verified Madurese equivalent is available.
- Avoid turning Pendalungan/Javanese-Indonesian mixed speech into "standard Madurese" without context.

## Generation Rules

1. Ask for dialect/community when the output must sound local.
2. Identify whether the audience is peers, elders, customers, public event attendees, or officials.
3. Use Madurese terms only when supported by local notes or user samples.
4. Keep uncertain official and technical terms in Indonesian.
5. Add a short review note for dialect, politeness, or public-use uncertainty.

## Starter Lexicon Policy

Do not bulk-copy dictionaries. Add only verified terms with dialect/register notes. Prefer full phrase examples because isolated words may not carry the right politeness level.
