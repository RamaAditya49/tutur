# Style Guide

## Language Identity

- Name: Bahasa Madura
- Region/context: Pulau Madura, Tapal Kuda Jawa Timur, Bawean, dan komunitas Madura diaspora
- ISO 639-3: `mad`
- Common Glottolog reference: `nucl1460`

## Register Position

Bahasa Madura is not a dialect of Bahasa Jawa. It has its own dialects and speech-level expectations. Before writing, ask whether the target is everyday, polite, Sumenep-style, Tapal Kuda/Pendalungan, Bawean, or another local context.

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent vocabulary.
- Do not assume one dialect represents all speakers.
- Do not import Javanese krama forms as if they were Madurese.
- Do not use casual forms for elders, formal events, public notices, or religious/cultural settings unless requested.
- Do not use the language for sacred, ritual, legal, or official text without review.
- Preserve Indonesian terms when the regional equivalent is unknown.
- Ask for a user-provided sample when voice or dialect matters.

## Practical Use

Use this skill for:

- source-aware regional-language output,
- tone adaptation,
- localized greetings or short public text,
- review of AI-generated local-language output,
- identifying uncertainty before publication.

## Practical Notes

- Ask for target region when the text is public.
- Be careful with terms of address, kinship, and politeness.
- For Tapal Kuda/Pendalungan contexts, clarify whether the desired voice is Madurese, Indonesian with Madurese flavor, or mixed local speech.
