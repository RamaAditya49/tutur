# Usage Patterns

## Ordinary Output

For everyday captions, short greetings, and review tasks, produce direct output when local references or user samples cover the wording. For dialect-specific or elder-facing text, ask for the target or mark the uncertainty.

## Sample Tasks

- Make everyday and polite variants of a short message.
- Review whether a text confuses Madurese with Javanese.
- Localize a Tapal Kuda community announcement with conservative wording.
- Flag Sumenep/Bawean/dialect terms that need review.
- Create a bilingual Indonesian-Madurese caption.

## Register And Semantics

- Everyday: peer/community tone; not automatically suitable for elders.
- Polite/public: use when addressing customers, elders, or event audiences.
- Sumenep-style prestige tone: do not use unless requested and supported.
- Pendalungan/Tapal Kuda: mixed social context; avoid presenting mixed Indonesian-Javanese-Madurese as standard.

## Review Checklist

- Is Madurese kept separate from Javanese?
- Is the dialect or community target stated?
- Are politeness choices suitable for the audience?
- Are official/technical terms handled conservatively?
- Are facts, names, dates, and places unchanged?

## Output Patterns

- Direct request: output first, brief note if dialect is uncertain.
- Review: dialect/politeness issues first, then safer wording.
- Public text: bilingual output plus speaker-review note for uncertain parts.
