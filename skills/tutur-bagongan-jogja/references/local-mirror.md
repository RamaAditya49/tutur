# Local Mirror

## Source Mirror

- Register: Yogyakarta/Jogja court-inspired Basa Bagongan.
- Court context: Keraton Kasultanan Yogyakarta; Pakualaman is related but not automatically identical.
- Main distinction: keep this separate from Surakarta Basa Kedhaton and ordinary Bahasa Jawa.
- Common local markers in this repo include the pronoun pair `manira` and `pakenira` when direct pronouns are appropriate.
- General KBBI/EYD references are useful only for Indonesian support text, not for validating Bagongan wording.

## Local Usability

Use this file before browsing. The skill can produce respectful Bagongan-inspired wording for cultural copy, event flavor, and review.

It must not claim authoritative palace protocol, ritual validity, inscription accuracy, or historical exactness. For those contexts, produce usable support text and mark the exact parts requiring Yogyakarta court-language expert review.

## Vocabulary And Semantics

- Basa Bagongan has a distinct court identity; it is not generic krama inggil.
- `manira` and `pakenira` are useful only when direct pronouns are needed and the context fits.
- A measured mix of krama and ngoko morphology can appear in Bagongan-style work, but it should not become random level mixing.
- Avoid importing Surakarta Kedhaton role-sensitive pronoun sets unless the task is comparative.
- Keep tone respectful, plain, and not artificially ancient.

## Generation Rules

1. Identify whether the target is Keraton Jogja/Kasultanan, Pakualaman, or a general cultural Jogja tone.
2. Choose light, Bagongan, or ceremony-aware depth.
3. Use `manira`/`pakenira` sparingly and only when pronouns are needed.
4. Preserve names, titles, dates, places, and facts.
5. Add a review note for ceremony, protocol, ritual, inscription, or historical contexts.

## Starter Lexicon Policy

Use `lexicon.md` as the local starter list. Do not invent palace vocabulary. Do not expand the lexicon without a reliable source or expert-reviewed sample.
