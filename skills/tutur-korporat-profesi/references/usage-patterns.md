# Usage Patterns

## Ordinary Output

For everyday corporate messages, produce the message directly. Add notes only when the wording depends on policy, legal approval, salary, refund, public crisis, or sensitive employee/customer data.

## Sample Tasks

- Rewrite sales follow-up after proposal.
- Write CS response for a delayed order or service issue.
- Make admin reminder for document submission.
- Write HRD interview invitation or rejection.
- Turn messy internal chat into a crisp project update.
- Make invoice reminder firm but polite.
- Convert corporate jargon into clear Indonesian.

## Register And Semantics

- WhatsApp sales: warmer and shorter than email.
- Email HRD: structured, respectful, complete.
- Internal Slack/WA: action-first, low ceremony.
- Customer complaint: empathy before explanation.
- Finance reminder: amount, date, invoice number, next action.
- Leadership update: status, risk, ask, owner, deadline.

## Review Checklist

- Is the role clear?
- Is the channel clear?
- Is there one next step?
- Are facts, amounts, dates, policy, and commitments accurate?
- Is the tone appropriate for customer/candidate/internal audience?
- Does it avoid fake urgency, blame, and empty jargon?

## Output Patterns

- Direct request: provide the message first.
- Review request: role/tone risks first, then rewrite.
- Multi-role request: label variants by role and channel.
- Sensitive topic: provide safe wording plus a policy-check note.
