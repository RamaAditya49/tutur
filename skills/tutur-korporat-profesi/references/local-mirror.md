# Local Mirror

## Source Mirror

This local mirror turns the research into an offline writing guide:

- KBBI/EYD/PUPI: standard Indonesian, spelling, diction, and term formation.
- KBBI `ragam bahasa`: language varies by topic, relationship, and medium.
- Plain-language guidance: business/public messages should be clear enough to understand and use.
- Sales references: soft selling builds trust, hard selling uses urgency; objection handling starts with listening and acknowledging.
- CS references: complaint handling needs empathy, sincere apology, neutral explanation, action, and follow-up.
- HR references: candidate communication should be prompt, personalized when possible, concise, and respectful.

## Local Usability

Use this file when writing for a specific profession or corporate function. It does not replace company policy, CRM scripts, legal approval, HR policy, or brand voice.

The skill may directly write ordinary workplace messages. For salary, termination, refund, legal, disciplinary, medical, or public-crisis topics, keep wording factual and ask for policy details.

## Vocabulary And Semantics

- `Follow up`: acceptable in sales/startup chat; in formal Indonesian use `menindaklanjuti`.
- `Closing`: sales decision/commitment; do not imply payment unless that is the next step.
- `Lead/prospek`: use `prospek` for formal Indonesian, `lead` for CRM/startup context.
- `Issue/kendala`: use `kendala` for customer-facing messages; `issue` is acceptable in internal tech/product chat.
- `Escalate`: use `eskalasi` internally; customer-facing text should say the case is being forwarded to the relevant team.
- `Offering`: HR context for offer; formal Indonesian is `penawaran kerja`.
- `Onboarding`: use as corporate term; explain as `proses awal bergabung` for broader audiences.

## Generation Rules

1. Start from the business goal, not from generic politeness.
2. Match the role and channel before choosing vocabulary.
3. Keep messages short enough to act on.
4. Use one clear next step.
5. Add deadline, owner, or required document when needed.
6. Remove filler such as "mohon maaf sebelumnya apabila mengganggu waktunya" unless context really needs it.
7. Keep empathy concrete, not theatrical.

## Profession Map

- Sales: warm, specific, benefit-led, question-based, no pressure unless urgency is real.
- Customer service: calm, accountable, empathetic, procedural, never defensive.
- Admin: precise, orderly, deadline-aware, document-aware.
- HRD/recruitment: respectful, clear, policy-aware, candidate-experience aware.
- Finance/billing: firm, polite, amount/date/invoice-focused, no shaming.
- Marketing: audience-led, benefit-specific, no hype without proof.
- Leadership: concise, decision-oriented, risk-aware, owner/deadline clear.
